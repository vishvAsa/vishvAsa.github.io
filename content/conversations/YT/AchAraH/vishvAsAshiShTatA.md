> Also, on a lighter but still sincere note what’s with the French bread in your videos? What kind of anushtana permits that? - @prathamkare694

With such questions, please clarify the intent of the question and the source of your objection to practice X.

**Education intent**  
If your intention is to educate me or yourself about practice X,  
you may, at the outset itself, cite the relevant pramANa (say shAstra vAkya or shiShTAchAra);  
and I will consider it.

I have unorthodox views about several shAstra injunctions.  
In case of shiShTAchAra too, I don't believe in following shiShTa-s to the extent of how one's nose is to be picked etc..  
Or it could just be my incapacity to adhere to some practice even where I want to.  
Or it could even be an unitnentional error.  
Practice X could be one of those.  


**Qualification challenge**  
On the other hand, if your intention is to question my qualification to make these videos pointing out fraud and mistakes in arguments, the below applies.

If you don't like practice X (due to some violation of "proper anuShThAna" or otherwise), don't imitate it.  
I don't claim to be perfect in anuShThAna.  
That doesn't disqualify me from pointing out lies or disagreeing with certain arguments.

**shiShTatva**  
Even if I did agree that I am some sort of a shiShTa, or if I were your AchArya,  
unlike shrI shauri-rAja (the publisher of the fraudulent sadanuShThAna-darpaNa),  
I would expect people concerned to remember the shruti "यान्य् अस्माकं सुचरितानि, तानि त्वयोपास्यानि नो इतराणि …", and follow only practices consonant with superior pramANa-s for dharma.
