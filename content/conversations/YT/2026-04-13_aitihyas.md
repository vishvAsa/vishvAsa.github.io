<details><summary>विस्तारः (द्रष्टुं नोद्यम्)</summary>

Just like you believe in your paramparā, we believe in ours. Jagadguru Srí Vidyaranya, who stands in the direct lineage of Jagadguru Srímad Ādi Śaṅkarācārya, has himself included in the Śaṅkarācārya Aṣṭottara the nāma “सौन्दर्यलहरीमुख्यबहुस्तोत्रविधायकाय नमः”, clearly acknowledging works like Soundarya Lahari. Many stotras not exclusively centered on Viṣṇu have indeed been composed by Bhagavadpāda. “तापनीय-रहस्यानां सारः कामासिकाहरिः” by Vedanta Desika  is accepted within your tradition, while the Nrisimha Tapaniya Upanishad itself uses names such as nīlalohita and pinākahasta, attributed to Parameśvara, pointing toward Hari Hara abheda.

If questioning stotras that have come down through a paramparā is taken as a basis to retrofit and challenge my entire belief system, which I hold to be true, then by the same logic one could claim that Yamunacharya never composed Stotra Ratna, Kulasekhara Alvar never composed Mukunda Mala, or that Vedanta Desika did not compose Paduka Sahasram after all, how could someone compose a thousand verses in just a few hours? You would not accept such claims even for a moment, because you trust your paramparā and your ācāryas.

Therefore, stotras like Śivānanda Lahari or Soundarya Lahari cannot be rejected merely because they do not align with a particular narrative, while expecting your own tradition’s accounts to be accepted without question. Either paramparā is a valid pramāṇa, or everything becomes selectively negotiable. When there is apparent confusion in śāstra, we follow “महाजनो येन गतः स पन्थाः”, taking śiṣṭācāra as pramāṇa.

As Advaitins, we do not discriminate between Parameśvara and Viṣṇu. When we respond strongly in favor of Parameśvara, it is only to establish firm ground and clarity, not to diminish Viṣṇu in any way. Śālagrāma ārādhana itself is part of pañcāyatana pūjā. Bhagavadpāda has composed stotras on Raṅganātha, Rāma, and Kṛṣṇa. Saints like Ramakrishna Paramahamsa and Tyagaraja have repeatedly emphasized that there should be no discrimination between devatās. “वृथा कण्ठक्षोभः” this is merely a futile agitation of the throat.

Especially at a time like this, when followers of Sanātana Dharma need to remain organized and united, finding fault with another siddhānta in an attempt to establish one’s own superiority particularly on public platforms where it can be exploited by those who seek to divide us is something that ought not to be done.
